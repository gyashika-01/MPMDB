Aloe-Vera (Aloe barbadensis Mill.)

Aloe-vera is a plant species that has been used for centuries for its medicinal and cosmetic properties. It is known for its succulent leaves, which contain a gel-like substance that is commonly used in various products. The gel extracted from aloe-vera leaves is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. It has soothing and moisturizing properties, which make it a popular ingredient in skincare and haircare products.

Aloe-vera gel is often used to treat sunburn, moisturize dry skin, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. Some people believe that drinking aloe-vera juice can help with digestion, boost the immune system, and improve overall health. However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the potential benefits and risks of consuming aloe-vera internally.



Nutritional Value of Aloe-vera :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Vitamins:Aloe-vera gel contains vitamins A, C, and E

  • Minerals:Aloe-vera gel contains several minerals, including calcium, magnesium, zinc, and selenium.

  • Polysaccharides:Aloe-vera gel contains complex carbohydrates called polysaccharides. These compounds have been associated with anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects.


Properties of Aloe-vera :


  • Aloe-vera gel has excellent moisturizing properties, which can help hydrate and soothe the skin. It is commonly used in skincare products like lotions, creams, and moisturizers.

  • Aloe-vera has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the skin. It may provide relief from conditions such as psoriasis, eczema, and acne by reducing redness, swelling, and itching.

  • Aloe-vera contains antioxidants, such as vitamins C and E, that can help protect the skin from oxidative damage caused by free radicals.

  • The polysaccharides found in aloe-vera gel may help stimulate the immune system and enhance its response to infections.

  • Aloe-vera gel is often consumed orally as a juice or supplement. It is believed to have a soothing effect on the digestive system and may help relieve symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders, such as acid reflux, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and constipation

  • Aloe-vera contains compounds with antimicrobial properties that can help fight against certain bacteria and fungi. It may be used topically to prevent or treat skin infections.


Side Effects of Aloe-vera :


  • Aloe-vera can cause skin irritation or contact dermatitis in some people.

  • Consuming aloe-vera orally, especially in large amounts, may cause digestive discomfort such as diarrhea, abdominal cramps, or electrolyte imbalances.

  • If a person have a known allergy to plants in the Liliaceae family (such as onions, garlic, or tulips), then he/she more likely to have an allergic reaction to aloe-vera.


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using aloe-vera with:

  • Diuretics (water pills):Aloe vera may have a diuretic effect, which means it can increase urine production.

  • Antidiabetic drugs:Aloe vera may lower blood sugar levels.

  • Anticoagulants (blood thinners):Aloe vera contains compounds that may have anticoagulant properties and may interfere with the effectiveness of certain blood-thinning medications like warfarin (Coumadin) or aspirin. This can increase the risk of bleeding or affect blood clotting.
