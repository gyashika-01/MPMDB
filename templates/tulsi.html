Tulsi(Ocimum tenuiflorum)

Tulsi, also referred to as holy basil or Indian basil, is frequently referred to as the "Queen of Herbs" in India. It is grown in almost every Hindu household and is regarded as a holy herb. Tulsi is revered not only for its holiness but also for its prowess as a powerful healer.



Nutritional Value of Tulsi :


    Nutritional components of tulsi:

  • Vitamins:Tulsi is an excellent source of a number of vitamins, including vitamin A, vitamin C, vitamin K, and vitamin B-complex vitamins including vitamin B6 and riboflavin.

  • Minerals:Tulsi includes a number of important minerals, including calcium, magnesium, potassium, iron, and manganese. These minerals are necessary for many body processes, such as energy metabolism, bone health, and neuron and nerve cell function.

  • Anti-Oxidants:Tulsi includes a number of antioxidants, including flavonoids and phenolic substances like eugenol. These anti-oxidants aid in defending the body against free radicals, which can harm cells and exacerbate chronic illnesses.

  • Essential Oils:Tulsi contains essential oils, which contribute to its unique flavour and scent. Eugenol and ursolic acid, two components of these oils, have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory activities.

  • PhytoChemicals:Tulsi includes a variety of phytochemicals, including as luteolin, apigenin, and rosmarinic acid. The potential anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and immune-modulating properties of these substances have been investigated.


Medicinal Uses of Tulsi:


  • Tulsi is used to treat kidney stones, the common cold, sore throat, fever, and headaches.

  • Blackheads, acne, and premature ageing are all conditions that can be treated with tulsi leaves.

  • Insect bites can be treated with tulsi.

  • Additionally, heart disease and respiratory issues are treated with tulsi.

  • Blood is purified by tulsi.

  • Tulsi cures colds and flu when chewed.

  • Two times day, chew 12 basil leaves to reduce tension.

  • The leaves strengthen the nerves and improve memory.

  • Bronchitis, asthma, influenza, cough, and cold can all be treated with a decoction of the leaves combined with honey and ginger.

  • Taking basil leaf juice daily for six months will cause kidney stones to pass via the urinary system.


Properties of Tulsi:


  • It might be an antipyretic (fever-relieving) substance.

  • It could be anti-inflammatory in nature.

  • An antiemetic, which stops vomiting, could be present.

  • It might be an anti-diabetic that lowers blood sugar.

  • It might lower blood pressure by acting as a hypotensive.

  • Hypolipidemic (lowering cholesterol) activity may be present.

  • It might reduce pain by acting as an analgesic.

  • Potentially anti-asthmatic properties.


Side Effects of Tulsi :


  • Tulsi may have an impact on a woman's ability to become pregnant.

  • When first introducing tulsi tea to your diet, some people experience nausea or diarrhoea, so it's best to start out slowly and increase your intake over time.

  • If you have diabetes and are taking medication to lower your blood sugar, you should use tulsi with caution.


Interactions with other Drugs :


  • In patients receiving treatment with drugs metabolised by CYP2B6 (artemisinin, cyclophosphamide, ketamine, efavirenz, methadone, and bupropion) or with rifampicin, tulsi may result in herb-drug interactions. As a result, care must be taken when combining these drugs with tulsi.
