Cinnamon(Cinnamomum verum)

Cinnamon is a spice derived from the inner bark of trees from the Cinnamomum family. It has been used for centuries for its distinctive flavor and aroma, as well as its potential health benefits.It has been studied for its potential role in helping regulate blood sugar levels. It may improve insulin sensitivity, increase glucose uptake by cells, and reduce insulin resistance.

However, it's important to note that cinnamon should not replace proper medical care or treatment for diabetes, and individuals with diabetes should consult with their healthcare provider before making any dietary changes.



Nutritional Value of Cinnamon :


    The nutrients found are:

  • Calories:6

  • Protein:0.1 grams

  • Fat:0.1 grams

  • Manganese:0.5 milligrams (25% of the Daily Value)

  • Vitamin K:0.4 micrograms

  • Iron:0.2 milligrams


Properties of Cinnamon :


  • Anti-Oxidant Activity:Cinnamon is rich in antioxidants, such as polyphenols. These compounds help protect the body against oxidative stress caused by free radicals, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases.

  • Anti-Inflammatory Effects:Some studies suggest that cinnamon may have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the body.

  • Anti-Microbial Properties:Cinnamon contains natural antimicrobial compounds that can inhibit the growth of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms. This property has made cinnamon a popular natural preservative and spice in traditional medicine.

  • Potential Heart Health Benefits:Some studies suggest that cinnamon may have positive effects on heart health. It may help lower LDL (bad) cholesterol, triglyceride levels, and blood pressure, which are risk factors for cardiovascular diseases.


Side Effects of Cinnamon :


  • Blood Sugar Control:While cinnamon has been studied for its potential benefits in blood sugar control, it may also interact with diabetes medications.

  • Coumarin Content:Cinnamon contains a compound called coumarin, which in high doses can be harmful to the liver and may cause liver damage.

  • Skin Sensitivity:Direct contact with cinnamon powder or oil may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals.

  • Allergic Reactions:Some individuals may be allergic or sensitive to cinnamon. Allergic reactions can vary from mild to severe and may include symptoms like skin rashes, itching, swelling, or difficulty breathing.


Interactions with other Drugs :


    It is essential to take precautions when using Cinnamon with:

  • Calcium Channel Blockers:Some studies suggest that cinnamon may lower blood pressure.

  • Liver-Metabolized Drugs:Cinnamon contains coumarin, which can be metabolized by the liver.

  • Anticoagulant/Antiplatelet Drugs:Cinnamon contains a compound called coumarin, which may have blood-thinning properties.

  • Cytochrome P450 Interactions:Cinnamon may interact with enzymes in the cytochrome P450 family, which are responsible for metabolizing various medications in the liver.
